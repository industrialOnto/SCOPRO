Introduction back to ToC

SCONTO describes the supply chain (SC) by extending and formalizing the SCOR model. It captures the SC structure, its associated business processes and associated resources, as well as its performance evaluation and benchmarking systems. The concepts of the SCOPRO subontology are organized around three dimensions: structure, processes, and resources, emphasizing the SC processes knowledge. SCOPRO provides the following capabilities:
  • To describe supply chains that are composed of one or more enterprises.
  • To represent the SC structure and the organizations that participate in it.
  • To specify organizational processes and their decompositions into interrelated subprocesses.
  • To define atomic and composite temporal relationships between processes and process components.
  • To describe a variety of resources, the effects that activities have on them, and the multiple roles they can play in the SC processes.
  • To explicitly represent the transportation/movement of materials between SC partners.
This representation establishes a common vocabulary for all the SC stakeholders, general enough to be employed in supply chains of different types of industries and having dissimilar sizes. However, SCOPRO can be extended to capture the characteristics of particular supply chains. SCOPRO has been fully developed, implemented, and evaluated. The other two subontologies, SCOME (Supply Chain Ontology for measurement and Evaluation) and SCOBE (Supply Chain Ontology for Benchmarking), which rely on SCOPRO basic concepts, still need further development.
